Seasonality, Trends and Noise

Here, the time series datasets have been broken down into their components: seasonality, trend and noise. Seasonality refers to a repeating short term cycle in a time series, trend refers to a long term increase or decrease in a dataset over time and noise refers to random variation in the time series .

Autocorrelation

Autocorrelation is the correlation of a signal with a delayed copy of itself as a function of the time lag between them. The plots below shows that light intensity, journey duration via car and bus are all highly correlated every 24 hours.

Predictive Model

A predictive model was built that is able to predict journey duration via bus based on the current light intensity. The model was built using a recurrent neural network (RNN) which is a class of artificial neural networks (ANN) designed to recognise sequential characteristics of a dataset in order to predict future scenarios based on past scenarios. The model was trained on 90% of the dataset and tested on the last 10%.
